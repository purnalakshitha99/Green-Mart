WHAT IS CYBERBULLYING?

Cyberbullying is a form of bullying that occurs online or through digital communication. It involves using technology, such as social media, texting, or messaging apps, to harass, intimidate, or humiliate someone. Cyberbullying can take many forms, including spreading rumors or lies about someone, posting embarrassing photos or videos, or sending threatening messages. The anonymity and distance provided by the internet can make cyberbullying particularly damaging, as it can be difficult to identify the perpetrator and the bullying can spread quickly to a wide audience. Cyberbullying can have serious consequences for the victim, including emotional distress, social isolation, and even physical harm. It is important to recognize the signs of cyberbullying and take steps to protect yourself and seek support if necessary.



   The most common places where cyberbullying occurs are :

Social Media, Such as Facebook , Instagram, Snapchat, and Tiktok.

Text messaging and messaging apps on mobile or tablet devices.

Instant messaging , direct messaging , and online chatting over the internet.

Online forums,chat rooms, and message boards, such as Reddit.

Email.

Online gaming communities.



   Types of CyberBullying:-



Harassment

Repeatedly sending hurtful or offensive messages, comments, or images to the victim. For example, consistently sending derogatory comments on someone's Instagram posts.

Flaming

Engaging in online arguments or fights with the intent to hurt someone emotionally. This could be a heated political debate that turns personal and hurtful.

Impersonation

Creating fake profiles or impersonating someone online to damage their reputation or deceive others. For instance, pretending to be a classmate and posting false, damaging information.

Outing

Sharing someone's personal, sensitive, or embarrassing information or media without their consent. This might involve posting private photos or messages publicly.

Exclusion

Purposefully leaving someone out of online groups, activities, or conversations to isolate them socially. This can happen in group chats or online gaming communities.

Doxing

Publishing someone's private or personal information, such as their address or phone number, with the intent to expose them to harm.

Cyberstalking

Persistently following, monitoring, or tracking someone online, often with threatening or obsessive behavior.

Verbal Abuse

Sending hurtful, threatening, or derogatory messages or comments to the victim. This could involve name-calling, insults, or offensive language.
