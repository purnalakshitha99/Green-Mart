CYBERBULLYING

    WHY DO PEOPLE CYBERBULLY?



There have always been people who choose to behave like a bully and those who are unfortunate targets. But what is it that makes one not just choose to bully another online, but to engage in the act in such a relentless and cruel manner? One theory is that cyberbullies have difficulty feeling empathy for others. Low empathy has certainly been attributed to increased use of technology and less real-world social interaction. But this is only one facet of the cyberbullying problem. Cyberbullying also allows a bully to feel more powerful. Through the use of technology, a cyberbully can easily and effortlessly unleash a torrent of torment at any time, all from the safe harbor of their own home, with little concern or repercussions. A lack of parental monitoring is another contributing factor, as is the desire to achieve popularity through acts they feel resonate well with their peers and associates. Regardless of the reasons, cyberbullies sometimes face their own dark futures.That kids who bully others are more likely to abuse drugs and alcohol, engage in fights and criminal behavior, drop out of school, or be abusive toward their romantic partners and their children. Even the bystanders and witnesses are not immune to the effects of cyberbullying. They may also have an increased likelihood of experimenting with drugs and alcohol at an early age or suffering emotional distress and mental health problems. As an interesting side note, one study states that cyberbullying is more likely to occur between current or former friends. The report further states that cyberbullying occurs seven times more frequently among teens who know each other than among people who had never been friends or dated. This often occurs due to a simple argument that escalates. Or it could simply be because one person has a secret about another that they are dying to tell. It could also be a case of revenge in response to some transgression the bully feels was committed. Jealousy is another factor, as is the desire to improve their social standing. In many of these cases, girls are twice as likely as boys to be victims of cyberbullying. And because the bully and the victim were once friends, it often results in the effects of cyberbullying being much more traumatic.


    IMPACTS OF CYBERBULLYING:-



Psychological distress

Cyberbullying can have a profound impact on the mental health of its victims, leading to various psychological distress symptoms. These can include feelings of worthlessness, hopelessness, anxiety, depression, and self-doubt. Additionally, individuals who experience cyberbullying may struggle with maintaining healthy relationships, sleeping habits, and engaging in pleasurable activities.

Academic difficulties

The prevalence of cyberbullying in schools and educational institutions can have significant implications for academic performance. Cyberbullying victims may struggle with concentrating, completing assignments, and engaging in class discussions. This, in turn, can result in decreased academic achievement and a higher risk of dropping out of school.

Physical health problems

Individuals who experience cyberbullying are at an increased risk of developing various health problems, including physical and mental health issues. Cyberbullying can exacerbate pre-existing anxiety, depression, and stress-related disorders, such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD). Moreover, cyberbullying can contribute to sleep disturbances, weight gain, and even more severe physical health problems, such as eating disorders and suicidal ideation. These symptoms can interfere with a victim's ability to concentrate, complete assignments, and maintain their overall well-being.

Social isolation

The persistent nature of cyberbullying can lead to feelings of isolation and social withdrawal among its victims. As a result, they may have difficulty maintaining healthy social connections, engaging in group activities, and participating in extracurricular events. This isolation can also contribute to feelings of loneliness, decreased self-esteem, and an increased vulnerability to other forms of emotional and physical distress.

Impact on relationships

Cyberbullying can have a negative impact on a victim's personal relationships, causing strain on friendships and family dynamics. This can lead to feelings of loneliness and social withdrawal.

Suicidal thoughts and behavior

The extreme nature of cyberbullying can place victims at a heightened risk of developing suicidal thoughts and ideation. In extreme cases, these thoughts can lead to actual suicide attempts or completed suicides. The potential consequences of cyberbullying extend far beyond the immediate individuals involved, encompassing the broader implications for mental health, well-being, and public safety.
